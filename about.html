About Me

Trained as an Architect, I got interested in Human-Centred Design (HCD) while I did my Master of Architecture thesis, which was about exploring possibilities of what and how to design leftover urban spaces by extracting open source Big Data. Ever since then, I've been creating experiences based on what people want and need.

Assumptions are basically
delayed failure.

My process usually kicks off from identifying of existing problems, validating it with a small group of people through research/testing, and investigating any available open source Big Data to re-confirm the problem's existence. I believe sketches are the most powerful thought process. I expect all of my results - failures and successes - to guide me to the next level.

When not I'm designing or learning coding, you can find me exploring museums or enjoying baked goods at local coffee shops.

Thanks for the visit.
